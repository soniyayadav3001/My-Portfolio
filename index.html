My Work

Ultra Translator

Ultra Translator is a Streamlit-based multilingual text and audio translation app using Google Translate and gTTS. Unlike Google Translate, it’s interactive, multi-modal, and customizable — supporting simultaneous multi-language translation, text-to-speech playback, real-time voice recording, and integration into custom apps or learning platforms. It enables real-time text, speech, and audio translations into 80+ languages and will soon support video translation.

Diwali Sales Analysis

An EDA project using Python (NumPy, Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn) in Jupyter/Colab to explore customer behavior, product preferences, and sales trends during Diwali. Load and preprocess the Diwali sales dataset (User_ID, Gender, Age/Age Group, Shaadi, State, Occupation, Product_Category, Orders, Amount), perform EDA (demographics, orders/sales by gender/age/state/occupation/category) and visualize with bar/count plots. Key findings: majority buyers are females 26–35, married women buy more, top states UP/MH/KA, top categories Food/Clothing/Electronics; next steps — forecasting, interactive dashboards (Streamlit/Power BI), clustering and multi-year analysis.

Employee Attrition Dashboard

A Power BI project analyzing employee data to uncover patterns behind attrition and support data-driven HR decisions. Using Power BI, DAX, and Excel/CSV, it visualizes attrition by department, role, gender, age, satisfaction, performance, and salary with interactive dashboards and drill-down insights. Key findings reveal high-risk departments/roles, links between satisfaction and attrition, and tenure trends; future goals include ML-based attrition prediction, feedback metrics, and real-time Power BI deployment.

Student Mental Health Risk Scorer

A simple Streamlit-based web app that helps students evaluate their mental health risk score based on factors like depression, anxiety, panic attacks, age, and CGPA. It collects student inputs through an interactive form, calculates a 0–100 risk score, and provides guidance as Low, Moderate, or High risk using a clean, user-friendly interface, the app aims to promote awareness

SONAR Rock vs. Mine Prediction

A Machine Learning project that classifies SONAR signals as rocks or mines using supervised learning. Built with Python, Scikit-learn, and Jupyter, it performs EDA, trains Logistic Regression, SVM, and Random Forest models, and achieves up to 92% accuracy (best with Random Forest). Future enhancements include deep learning integration, web app deployment (Flask/Streamlit), and optimized feature selection for improved performance.
